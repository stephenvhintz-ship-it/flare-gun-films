Logline

A quest for a crash site becomes a reckoning with legacy.

When a son and father embark on a quest to uncover the crash site of their WWII hero, they unearth not only the buried remains of a P-47 Thunderbolt, but the untold truths that will redefine their family's legacy.

Based on a true story, Finding Loren blends the scale of WWII aviation history with the quiet power of a father-son story. In 1945, Loren Hintz's P-47 was shot down over Italy, his remains lost for more than 70 years. Decades later, his son Martin and grandson Steve follow letters, maps, and local witnesses to uncover his final resting place.

The search bridges continents, generations, and the complex emotional terrain between fathers and sons - moving between Northwest Arkansas, Italy, archival memory, and the timeless call to finish the journeys our ancestors began.

Visual Language

Walton country to wartime Italy.

The feature is designed for a rich Southern visual language grounded in Northwest Arkansas, contrasted with the rugged intimacy of Italian recovery sites and the sweep of authentic WWII aviation imagery.

Documentary Foundation

The real search has already moved audiences.

The documentary Finding Loren follows the decades-long effort to recover Loren Hintz's remains and finally lay him to rest. Featuring narration from actor Dion Graham, it uses Hintz's own words, vintage photographs, archival footage, and family testimony to trace his life from Iowa farm boy to poet, husband, father, fighter pilot, and enduring family presence.

Tonal Comps

Lyrical, intimate, and epic.

Finding Loren blends the intimacy of A River Runs Through It and The Straight Story with the reflective war imagery of The Thin Red Line and Empire of the Sun. Like The Dig and Searching for Sugar Man, it is a quest for something lost - a personal excavation of history, sacrifice, and reconciliation.

Writer

Stephen Hintz

Stephen brings lived experience to Finding Loren, his most personal project to date. The story chronicles his and his father's real journey to uncover the crash site of his grandfather's P-47 Thunderbolt, lost in Italy for more than seventy years.

He co-wrote The Hill, which spent three weeks as Netflix's #1 film, and served as Executive Producer on Alison Eastwood's Battlecreek. His work lives at the intersection of history and the human heart.
